Gatekeeper Responsibilities

Implementation

This plan presupposes resources which do not exist today. Implementation should be considered phased, which is to say, as resources to put the plan into action become available the clauses here should be put into effect. Conditions which are known to require additional resources are marked with *.

Responsibilities

Code

  1. Contributors

    1. shall provide patches in context or unified format.
    2. should provide against the current head of whichever tree the patch is for.
    3. should provide a patch for both development and production trees where applicable.
    4. should provide a unit test for any contribution.(*)
    5. shall provide necessary assertions that the contribution can be legally contributed by them and are provided under an acceptable license.
    6. shall provide documentation for submissions which add commands or functionality or change user-visible behavior, meeting current documentation standards.(*)
  2. Gatekeepers

    1. may convene committees to delegate review and feedback to, but are ultimately responsible for enforcing project contribution standards.
    2. shall provide review of any architecture proposals submitted.
    3. shall insure an issue in the issue tracker is opened for all submissions.(*)
    4. shall copy all correspondence to the issue tracker.
    5. shall review all patches for compliance with OpenAFS code standards and provide feedback when standards are not met.
    6. shall review all patches for compliance with published protocol standards and provide feedback when standards are not met.
    7. may arrange for orphaned contributions deemed of value to the community to be adopted or stewarded until they meet needed standards.
    8. shall for major architectural changes engage the community for guidance and the Advisory Board for a final decision on acceptance. A review of the proposed changes shall be provided to the community and Advisory Board by the Gatekeepers.
    9. shall test submissions by compiling on all supported platforms before committing(*).
    10. shall apply all applicable unit tests before committing.(*)
    11. shall require current documentation standards to be applied.(*)
    12. shall note information about the committed change to the issue tracker.
    13. shall note which release(s) the change is targeted for to the issue tracker.
    14. shall keep an updated list of which changes have been accepted but not released and when they are expected in releases.(*)
    15. may keep a list of who has signed contributor agreements (possibly with issue tracker accounts for submitters) to simplify tracking of licensing of submissions.

Glossary

  1. A major architectural change is defined as one that
    1. requires additions, modifications, or deletion of communication protocols
    2. is deemed by the gatekeepers or the community to result in some undue amount of risk
    3. will require more than 40 hours to review and/or test
  2. An orphaned contribution is one for which
    1. the contributor has not responded to gatekeeper communications within six weeks.
